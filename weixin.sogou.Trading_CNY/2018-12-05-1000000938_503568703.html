石油人民币元Petroyuan崛起 以应对反复无常的关税制裁

路闻卓立编译 人民币交易与研究
人民币交易与研究

Trading_CNY

人民币交易与研究论坛由海内外从事交易、研究、监管的各方人士组成，旨在搭建市场参与者与政策监管层的交流平台、传递市场一线声音、以市场化力量推动中国金融市场进步及人民币国际化水到渠成。目前，平台已是中国最大的FICC领域深度信息提供商。

过去10年间，中国的人民币国际化战略，更多的依赖IMF的特别提款权(SDR)作为替代性国际储备货币。但今年推出的人民币计价石油期货交易表明，中国将正面追求"去美元化"。这也是应对反复无常的特朗普关税制裁的一种重要手段。


For the past decade, China’s strategy for internationalizing the renminbi has involved greater reliance on the IMF’s Special Drawing Rights as an alternative international reserve currency. But the launch of renminbi-denominated oil trading this year suggests that China will now pursue de-dollarization head-on.



中国在上海国际能源交易所（INE）推出以人民币计价的石油期货合约仅仅10个月。尽管有一些不确定和担忧，石油市场仍在继续运转，中国的期货合约已确立地位，交易量已超过新加坡和迪拜以美元计价的石油期货。


It is now just ten months since China launched its oil futures contract, denominated in yuan (renminbi), on the Shanghai International Energy Exchange. In spite of forebodings and shrill alarms, the oil markets continue to function, and China’s futures contracts have established themselves and overtaken in volume terms the dollar-denominated oil futures traded in Singapore and Dubai.


图片：SC1901原油1901自3月26日推出至今的行情  来源：新浪财经


当然，上海INE的交易量仍落后于伦敦交易的布伦特（Brent）原油合约和纽约交易的西德克萨斯中质原油期货（WTI）。然而，中国石油期货合约被跨国商品交易商（如嘉能可Glencore）认真对待，其定价方式堪比布伦特和WTI指数。正如我们在“亚太期刊”(Asia-Pacific Journal)上所言，这些结果表明，中国的石油期货可能会让人民币成为全球大宗商品市场的核心。


Of course the volume of trades on the Shanghai INE still lags behind that of the Brent oil contracts traded in London and the West Texas Intermediate oil futures traded in New York. 


The Chinese oil futures contract is, however, being taken seriously by multinational commodity traders (like Glencore) and is priced in a manner that is comparable to the Brent and WTI indices. As we argue in The Asia-Pacific Journal, these results suggest that China’s oil futures could bring the renminbi to the core of global commodity markets.


预计石油期货合约的推出，将扩大以人民币计价的大宗商品交易的范围。随着越来越多的中国石油进口以本币计价，外国供应商将拥有更多以人民币计价的账户，他们不仅可以用这些账户购买中国商品和服务，还可以购买中国政府的证券和债券。


这有望加强中国资本市场，促进人民币国际化——或者至少是石油市场逐步去美元化。


The launch of the oil futures contract can be anticipated to widen the scope for renminbi-denominated commodity trading. As more of China’s oil imports come to be priced in its domestic currency, foreign suppliers will have more renminbi-denominated accounts with which they can purchase not only Chinese goods and services, but also Chinese government securities and bonds. 

This can be anticipated to strengthen Chinese capital markets and promote the renminbi’s internationalization – or at least the progressive de-dollarization of the oil market.

过去10年，中国实现人民币国际化的战略，更多地依赖IMF的特别提款权(SDR)作为一种替代性国际储备货币。中国人民银行时任行长周小川，在2009年的一篇文章中阐述了这一战略。


随着特别提款权被重新分配给中国等新兴工业大国，以包括人民币在内的一篮子货币为基础的特别提款权，不仅可以作为一种发展工具，还可以作为一种与美元竞争的国际支付手段。2008年全球金融危机爆发后，以特别提款权为中心的国际金融体系也成为其他国家的魅力愿景。


For the past decade, China’s strategy for internationalizing the renminbi has involved greater reliance on the International Monetary Fund’s Special Drawing Rights as an alternative international reserve currency. The People’s Bank of China’s then-governor, Zhou Xiaochuan, spelled out the strategy in an essay in 2009. 


With new allocations of SDRs to emerging industrial powers like China, the SDR, based on a basket of currencies including the renminbi, could serve not only as a development tool, but also as a means of international payment to rival the US dollar. In the wake of the 2008 global financial crisis, an SDR-centered international financial system became an enticing prospect for other countries as well.


周小川在2009年的文章中指出了以美元为中心的体系的明显不足(例如美国长期赤字的影响)，并概述了特别提款权作为国际金融结算的替代手段的优势，从而推动了这些努力。


Zhou’s 2009 essay galvanized these efforts, as he pointed to the evident inadequacies of the dollar-centered system (such as the impact of chronic US deficits) and outlined the SDR’s advantages as an alternative means of international financial settlement.


在中国和许多其它经济体面临美国咄咄逼人的关税之际，建立以人民币计价的石油贸易，以及在其它大宗商品市场进一步发展以人民币计价的贸易，表明美元的霸权可能面临前所未有的挑战。


The establishment of renminbi-based oil trading at a time when China and many other economies confront aggressive US tariffs, and possible further development of renminbi-based trade in other commodity markets, suggests that the US dollar could face an unprecedented challenge to its hegemony. 


在不久的将来，美元可能不再被视为国际货币体系的支柱，终结前法国总统德斯坦(Valery Giscard d’estaing,1974-1981年任法国总统)所称的美国因美元在国际贸易中占据中心地位而享有的“过度特权”。


It may in the near future no longer be seen as the anchor of the international monetary system, bringing to an end to what Valéry Giscard d’Estaing famously called the “exorbitant privilege” enjoyed by the US as a result of the dollar’s centrality in international trade.


如果中国的最终目标是人民币国际化，那么其更直接的目标是国际体系的去美元化，这在一定程度上是由美国对中国和其他国家的关税或制裁推动的。这反映在推动上海石油交易期货合约的转变上，也代表了中国以特别提款权为重点的战略改变。


If China’s ultimate goals include internationalizing the renminbi, its more immediate objective, prompted in part by US tariffs or sanctions on China and other countries, is de-dollarization of the international system. This is reflected in the shift to promoting an oil futures contract traded in Shanghai, which represents a decisive break with China’s SDR-focused strategy.


这也是中国利用美国对其实施的贸易制裁的一种方式——展现反对制裁的责任。例如，俄罗斯和伊朗都在向中国出售石油，并接受人民币付款，以回应美国对他们实施的实际或潜在制裁。他们还从中国大量进口，以及寻求加强关系。


It is also a way for China to capitalize on the US trade sanctions imposed against it – exposing liabilities in these sanctions. Both Russia and Iran, for example, are selling oil to China and accepting payment in renminbi, in response to actual or potential sanctions imposed on them by the US. They also have extensive imports from China, as well as other reasons for seeking strengthened ties.


毫无疑问，中国将以人民币计价的石油合同的出现，视为中国企业以本国货币购买油气的一种手段，从而避免受外汇波动的影响，并向美元霸主地位发起挑战。


中国目前是全球最大的石油进口国，同时也是全球贸易和制造业的主要经济体，这一事实加大了中国“石油人民币”(petroyuan)，及其它人民币国际化举措的重要性。如果中国能够抵御美国的反击，它的努力将为多极化世界的出现奠定金融基础。


China no doubt views the emergence of renminbi-denominated oil contracts as a means for Chinese companies to buy oil and gas in their own currency, thereby avoiding exposure to foreign currency fluctuations and firing a shot across the bow of US dollar supremacy. 


The fact that China is now the world’s largest oil importer, as well as its leading trading and manufacturing economy, lends weight to its “petroyuan” and other initiatives to internationalize the renminbi. If China can withstand US counterattacks, its efforts will lay the financial groundwork for the emergence of a multipolar world.
