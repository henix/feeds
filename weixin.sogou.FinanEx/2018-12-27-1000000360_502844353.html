Peter Cziraki (University of Toronto)

Jasmin Gider (Tilburg University)


摘要

本文考察内部人交易赚到的钱数(dollar profits)，说明公司内部人是否会利用其信息优势。研究发现，内部人交易赚的钱在经济意义上很小，超额利润的中位数仅为464美元/年。以往用于预测收益率的变量不能用来预测利润值，表明具有信息优势的内部人并没有把这一优势变为利润。交易不频繁的内部人收益率高，而交易频繁的内部人利润值高。本文利用一个法律规定的断点来构建衡量内部人意图的新指标，这种方法可以预测利润值和收益率。该指标成功识别了一小部分(0.5%)利润值很高的内部人，中位数为2500美元/年。最后，用证监会预算的变化来考察监管力度能否解释收益率和利润值的不同分布。本文的研究说明，在内部人交易的诸多问题中，用利润值与用收益率的结果会不同。本文认为，验证代理理论用利润值更精确。


The Dollar Profits to Insider Trading


Abstract

This paper studies trade quantities and dollar profits to insider trading, to investigate whether corporate insiders can exploit their superior information about the firm. We find that dollar profits are economically small for a typical insider, the median insider earning abnormal profits of $464 per year. Variables that predict percentage returns fail to predict dollar profits, suggesting that insiders with the largest superior information do not turn this advantage into profits. Insiders who trade infrequently make high returns, while insiders who trade frequently make large dollar profits. We exploit a legally-imposed discontinuity to construct a new measure of insiders’ intentions, which predicts both dollar profits and returns. This measure successfully identifies a small subset (0.5%) of insiders whose profits are significantly higher with a median of $2,500 per year. Finally, we use variation in SEC budgets over time to assess whether monitoring can explain the different distribution of returns and profits. Our work highlights that using dollar profits as opposed to percentage returns offers contrasting evidence on a number of questions about insider trading. We argue that profits are a more precise measure for testing agency theories.


原文：

Cziraki, Peter and Jasmin Gider, The Dollar Profits to Insider Trading，2019 AFA Annual Meeting Working Paper, December 12, 2018.
